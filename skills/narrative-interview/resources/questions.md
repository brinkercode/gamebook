# Narrative Interview — Question Bank

---

## Section 1: Setting

1. **Where and when does this game take place?** (Physical location, era, and the one sentence that makes this world different from "generic future city")
2. **What event broke the world or set the story in motion?** (The inciting event that happened before the player arrives)
3. **What is the player walking into?** (State of the world at game start — not backstory, the immediate present)
4. **Are there factions?** If yes: name them, describe what each wants, and explain why they conflict.

---

## Section 2: Protagonist

5. **Who is the player character?** (Name, role, why they are the one doing this)
6. **What does the protagonist want at the start?** (Stated goal — what they think the game is about)
7. **What does the protagonist actually need?** (Thematic undercurrent — what the game is really about)
8. **Does the player character speak?** (Silent protagonist / voiced / text-only — this affects dialogue tree branching and recording budget)
9. **Is there a player-facing character customization?** (Name input, appearance — scope flag if yes)

---

## Section 3: Antagonist and Conflict

10. **Who or what is the antagonist?** (Person, system, environment, the protagonist's own nature)
11. **Does the antagonist have a point?** (The best antagonists are not wrong — describe their argument)
12. **Is the antagonist present in the vertical slice?** (Or only foreshadowed?)

---

## Section 4: Hook and Stakes

13. **What is the story hook — one sentence that makes the player need to know what happens next?** (Not "save the world" — a specific, personal, irreversible thing that forces forward motion)
14. **What does the player lose if they fail?** (Emotional stakes, not mechanical)
15. **Is there a twist or reversal planned?** (Not the content — just yes/no and which act it lands in)

---

## Section 5: Act Structure (Vertical Slice Only)

16. **Describe the opening scene in one paragraph.** (What the player sees and does in the first two minutes)
17. **What is the mid-point shift?** (The moment the goal changes or the world is revealed differently)
18. **How does the vertical slice end?** (Not the game — the playable slice. What is the last image?)

---

## Section 6: Content and Rating

19. **Target age rating?** (ESRB: E10+ / T / M / AO — or PEGI equivalent. This affects violence depiction, language, and sexual content in dialogue)
20. **What content is explicitly forbidden?** (Real-world hate symbols, certain real-world locations, specific real-world events — list them)
21. **Is there a content warning needed?** (Mental health themes, substance use, animal harm — does the game require an in-game content warning screen?)

---

## Section 7: Audio Log and Environmental Storytelling

22. **Are there collectible audio logs or readable documents?** (If yes: how many in the vertical slice? Who are the voices? What do they reveal that dialogue doesn't?)
23. **How much story is told through the environment itself?** (Dead bodies posed to imply events, graffiti, environmental props — is this a primary or secondary storytelling mode?)
